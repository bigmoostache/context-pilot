# GenAI France — Conference Vibe & Context

## The Conference

- **Name:** GenAI Days 2026 — "Agentic at Scale Conference"
- **Org:** Generative AI France (10,000+ members, 6 cities, 50+ events/year)
- **Date:** April 16, 2026, Paris (Maison internationale, 14e)
- **Format:** 1 day, 11 talks, 350+ attendees
- **Language:** Bilingual French/English (most talk titles in English)

## The Vibe

**Practical, REX-driven, company-backed.** This is not an academic conference — it's practitioners sharing what they actually built, what broke, what worked. Every speaker has a company logo behind them. The motto is literally *"partage d'expériences concrètes et de retours terrain authentiques."*

**Audience:** CTOs, product managers, data scientists, entrepreneurs, developers. Broad tech community, not just ML engineers. People who *use* AI in production, not just research it.

**Energy:** Networking-heavy. Coffee breaks, apéro communautaire, partners village. French tech ecosystem with international ambition.

**Tone:** Enthusiastic but grounded. Not hype-peddling. "Here's what we built, here's what happened, here's what we learned."

## How Our Talk Fits

**Unique positioning vs. the lineup:**
1. **Only DIY tool builder** — everyone else uses existing platforms (Lovable, Codex, Replit)
2. **Only Rust angle** — the entire conference is Python/TS/no-code; Rust is exotic here
3. **Infrastructure/harness focus** — not "we used AI to do X" but "here's how we made AI *reliable*"
4. **Self-hosting story** — the tool builds itself, compound returns over 766 commits
5. **Deep technical + clear business value** — 961 lints, hash chains, but also: velocity, cost savings

**Natural complements:**
- Quentin Pleplé's "Let your agent code while you sleep" — similar dream, different approach (ours is more disciplined/feedback-loop-driven)
- "Vibe coders vs code engineers" (2025) — we're firmly on the engineer side, with proof
- Katia Gil Guzman's OpenAI Codex talk — contrasts platform (Codex) vs. custom harness (ours)
