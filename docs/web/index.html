One agent per project.

Context Pilot is a team of AI agents you can leave in charge — research, writing, data, ops, whatever the project needs. Post one to each of yours, then step away: they keep working, checking their own results, and making progress. Check in from your terminal or your browser, whenever you like.

A COWORKER, NOT A CHATBOT

Delegate a project. Walk away. Come back to progress.

Working with Context Pilot feels less like prompting a tool and more like handing work to a teammate who keeps going after you log off.

  1. 01

    Post an agent to a project

    Point an agent at a project folder and hand it the work — research to gather, a document to draft, a backlog to clear. It sets up its own workspace and gets going, no babysitting required.

  2. 02

    Step away

    Head into a meeting, or start another project of your own. Your agents keep working and checking their own results, pausing only when they genuinely need a decision from you.

  3. 03

    Check in from anywhere

    Glance at the whole team from the browser cockpit, or drop into any single agent from your terminal. Review, nudge, redirect — then leave them to it again.

PLUG-IN APPLIANCE

Or run the whole team from a little box.

Context Pilot runs on the Photonicat — a pocket-sized 5G Wi-Fi router. Plug it in, join its network, and your agents are working on a private LAN in minutes. No server to rack, no cloud to trust, nothing to configure.

Its own 5G uplink means it works anywhere there's signal — a new office, a client site, a kitchen table. Drop one on a home or company network and the whole team shares it.
